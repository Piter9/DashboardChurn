Customer Churn Analysis


Case Study: Telecommunication Company

Introduction

Background

Customers are one of the key indicators any companies should pay attention to. Churn refers to the phenomenon in which customers stop using a company's products or services. Churn can be a major problem for companies, leading to increased new customer acquisition costs (CAC) and reduced customer loyalty, ultimately jeopardizing overall business success.


Churn rate is a key metric that allows companies to measure the percentage of customers lost over a specific period. Churn rate can be calculated by using the following formula

$$ \text{Churn Rate} = \frac{\text{Number of Customers Lost during Period}}{\text{Total Number of Customers at the Start of Period}} \times 100 $$


The ideal churn rate for mature and established companies is 5% to 7% in annual churn and less than 1% in monthly churn (according to article in forbes.com). However, measuring churn solely through churn rate can lead to misleading conclusions about company's overall customer retention challenges.

Late Response

Churn rates can only be measured after the period ends so they can only react to churn after it happens, missing the opportunity to intervene early and retain at-risk customers.

Learn More
Lack of Customer Insight

Customer churn can be caused by various things. A simple churn rate formula only provides a single overall number, which does not give insight into why customers are leaving or which specific customers are most likely to churn.

Learn More
Limited Predictive Capabilities

Traditional methods (churn rate formula) can not identify those who are likely to churn in the future. Thus, companies can not forecast the upcoming churn trends, which limits their ability to adjust strategies proactively.

Methodologies

What We Do?

Customer Profiling

A cluster-based analysis by categorizing customers into certain groups based on the characteristics of each customer.


Churn Prediction

A machine learn modeling to predict whether customer is most likely to churn or not churn based on their characteristics, such as customer identification, service used, charges, etc

Sources

Dataset Used

For the analysis, we use dataset of a telecommunication company which provided home phone and internet services in California, USA. In summary, the dataset contains 7.043 customer records in Q3 period and 50 attributes as the customer characteristics, including 4 categories:

Customer Identification

Customer identity such as gender, age, marital status, dependents, and status in society

Data Source

Services Used

Services subscribed by customers such as type of internet services, telephone, streaming, security plan, add-on services, and subscription type

Data Source

Customer Expenses

Information related to expenses and payments by customers such as total expenses, total returns, payment methods, total revenue, total projected customer value by the company, and average service usage by customers

Data Source

Customer Status

The status of customers in the company at the end of the period includes churn status, reasons for churn (if churn), and customer satisfaction level.

Enhance Customer Experience

Enhanced Customer Experience: By analyzing churn patterns, companies can identify pain points in the customer journey and improve their overall experience. This could involve streamlining processes, enhancing support services, or providing better onboarding experiences.
